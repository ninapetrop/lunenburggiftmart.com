"Giftmart a Success!"

Dec. 19, 2015 -Luenburg Ledger Letter to the Editor

"The St. Boniface church hall was filled with hundres of unwrapped gifts allowing over 100 families to shop for their holiday gift giving at no cost. Donations included toys for all ages, board games, crafts, warm clothing, sporting goods, and home décor, to name some of the items. “It’s heartwarming to experience the generosity of the community”, states committee member Kathy Murray. The committee starts setting up 3 days prior to the event, organizing the donations for easy shopping and to ensure that all guests have the opportunity to take something home. "

"Boy Scouts help sort donated toys"

Dec. 16, 2012 -Sentinel & Enterprise

WORCESTER -- Several Cub Scouts from Lunenburg Pack 1728 worked alongside the 25th Marine Regiment at Toys for Tots on Dec. 8. The Cub Scouts traveled to 10 New Bond St. to help separate and bag toys for children.

They volunteered with the added benefit of knowing they were helping their local community when some of the toys separated and bagged went to Gift Mart in Lunenburg. Gift Mart has been helping families in need for many years now, and their one-day grand opening was Saturday.

Thank you for supporting Giftmart

Dec. 19, 2015 -Luenburg Ledger Letter to the Editor

To The Editor - The Lunenburg Giftmart committee would like to thank everyone who helped with the December 18 event. Giftmart is the perfect example of our community working together. Lunenburg PTO sponsors the event and St. Boniface hosts, but we also work with Toys for Tots, Lunenburg lions, Community COuncil, Lunenburg Police, girl scouts, and each of our schools contributed. We also would like to thank New Outlooks, Lunenburg Library, and Chester Mossman Teen center for collecting the donations.
